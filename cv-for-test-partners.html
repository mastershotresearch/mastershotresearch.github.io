Work experience

Senior User Researcher for Homes England

01/2020–Today

I am the user researcher of Scrum team that aims to make the data that Homes England produces available to all employees that need it. I am helping out my team by finding out a) what information each employee needs to carry out their job; b) how users currently go about gathering that information; c) what new pieces of information would make their jobs easier. To find that out, I’m mainly using contextual interviews — learning how users work by being close to them.

Senior User Researcher for the Department of Work and Pensions

01/2019–1/2020

I was the user researcher of a Scrum team that was building a program called Searchlight, which is used by more than 80,000 employees from different government departments. When I joined the team, the program was in private beta, and when I left it was fully functional and available to everyone. My job consisted in providing insights to the team so that we could progress confidently during that process. I used the following research methods: contextual interviews with more than 40 users; a survey with more than 3,000 respondents; 6 rounds of usability tests; 3 concept value tests; 1 content analysis of the feedback received from users; and 1 tree test with more than 200 users. To document the research done, I’ve created written reports, slides, and journey maps.
